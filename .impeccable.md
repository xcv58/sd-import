## Design Context

### Users

SD Import is for photographers and video creators who need to copy card media
quickly and confidently after shoots. They are often working with removable
media, large files, repeated card names, and time-sensitive handoff workflows.

### Brand Personality

Quiet, capable, and trustworthy. The interface should feel like a paid Mac app:
native, organized, fast to scan, and friendly without becoming chatty.

### Aesthetic Direction

Use a restrained macOS utility style closer to Finder, Photos, and Image
Capture than a flashy creative app. Prefer native materials, clear hierarchy,
system icons, compact controls, and semantic status colors. Avoid marketing
layouts, decorative gradients, oversized illustrations, and novelty styling.

### Design Principles

- Put workflow confidence first: source, destination, counts, and warnings must
  be easy to verify before copying.
- Keep the interface quiet and scannable: labels should be short, alignment
  consistent, and advanced choices available without visual noise.
- Respect native macOS conventions: menu commands, sidebars, toolbars, segmented
  controls, system materials, and semantic colors should do most of the work.
- Make potentially destructive or irreversible actions visually distinct and
  clearly named.
- Polish for repeated use: reduce friction, preserve density, and make keyboard
  shortcuts discoverable without turning the app into a tutorial.
